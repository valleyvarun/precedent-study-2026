Scratch is a block-based visual programming language and global online community designed to teach children the fundamentals of coding through interactive storytelling, games, and animations. The project was conceived in 2003 by Mitchel Resnick and the Lifelong Kindergarten Group at the MIT Media Lab, launching publicly in 2007 with backing from the National Science Foundation. It was created to provide a highly accessible, playful environment where kids could develop creative thinking, systemic reasoning, and collaborative skills without getting bogged down by complex typed syntax. Today, the platform is now owned and managed by the Scratch Foundation, an independent non-profit organization. It is incredibly popular, boasting well over 100 million registered users globally and standing as the world's most widely used introductory programming platform.
