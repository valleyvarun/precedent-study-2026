Resnick's 4 P's for learning creative thinking:

  • Project

    People learn best when actively building something from start to finish. Creating a complete project teaches problem-solving and idea expression, rather than just isolated programming skills.

  • Passion

    People work harder and dive deeper when they care about what they are making. Connecting learning to personal interests fuels motivation learn.

  • Peers

    Learning is a social process. Collaborating, sharing ideas, receiving feedback, and helping others, builds critical teamwork skills.

  • Play

    Creativity requires a playful mindset. It is about experimenting, taking risks, and relying on trial and error without the fear of making mistakes and breaking things.

In the 1990s and early 2000s, there was no way to code visual graphics without first learning lengthy and complex language syntax.


Seymour Papert believed that kids could learn and understand computational concepts much better if the output of their programs was visual. To put this into practice, he created Logo, a programming language with simple commands that controlled the movement of a character to draw shapes mathematically.


Mitchel Resnick worked on the LEGO Mindstorms project, which was a robotics kit featuring programmable blocks with motors and sensors. While putting the blocks together to make the robots was simple and intuitive, programming them was still very difficult for the intended users. Mitchel felt the project was incomplete without a programming language that was as simple as putting these LEGO blocks together.
